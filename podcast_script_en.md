Speaker 1: Welcome to Retbaa Circle Insider. This podcast is your space — for the investors in Retbaa's private circle. A candid conversation about what's really happening inside Retbaa, beyond the slide decks. I'm Alex.

Speaker 2: And I'm Kemia, Retbaa's strategic analyst. Today we're talking about money — the 2025 numbers, and what the next three years could look like. But we're going to try to tell this as a story, not as a spreadsheet.

Speaker 1: So let's start at the beginning. 2025 — Retbaa's first real operating year. Kemia, what's the honest picture?

Speaker 2: The honest picture is that Retbaa came out of the gate fast. In barely six months of concentrated activity — because a lot of 2025's energy went into building foundations, not just selling — Retbaa generated 191,000 euros in revenue. And what struck me most when I looked at the accounts is that the company is already profitable. Positive net income. At this stage, for a brand that just launched, that's worth pausing on.

Speaker 1: Profitable in year one. You hear a lot of founders say "we'll get there in five years." Massata is delivering results from day one.

Speaker 2: And it's not luck. It's the result of one very deliberate strategic choice: never burden the business with heavy fixed costs. No long-term retail lease, no factory, no oversized payroll. Production happens on demand, from a logistics hub in Dakar. That's the asset-light model — and in 2025, it produced a 22.7% EBITDA margin. To put that in perspective: that's close to Hermès-level margins. On a startup.

Speaker 1: That's the past. What matters for us as investors is what comes next. Who is buying Retbaa today, and can this actually become something significant?

Speaker 2: The customers today are primarily corporations — B2B. Premium corporate gifting, hotels, institutional buyers across West Africa. And that's actually the best possible foundation for a nascent luxury brand: recurring clients, stable volumes, credibility that builds organically. E-commerce and direct consumer sales represent less than 5% of revenue today. Which means the biggest part of the growth story hasn't even started yet.

Speaker 1: And that's where the fundraising comes in. To those of you listening — you invested in Tranche 1, which closed at 360,000 euros on February 5th, 2026. Tranche 2 is now open at 240,000 euros. Kemia, what does this capital actually do?

Speaker 2: It converts what already exists. There are 800,000 euros in qualified commercial opportunities waiting right now — identified contracts, advanced conversations. This capital is the catalyst that turns them into revenue. And there's a strategic layer on top: closing this tranche unlocks up to 600,000 euros in non-dilutive financing — public subsidies through BPI France, Proparco, and CCI Paris. Meaning the euro you invest can potentially unlock another euro for free.

Speaker 1: So we're talking about a total deployment of 1.2 million euros, half of which doesn't dilute shareholders.

Speaker 2: Exactly. It's a very intelligent financing structure. And quite rare at this stage.

Speaker 1: Let's talk about the trajectory now. 2026, 2027, 2028 — the projections. Massata is targeting 1.5 million euros in revenue over three years. Is that grounded in reality?

Speaker 2: The question is always: is this built on something, or is it optimism? In this case, it's built on something. 2026: 500,000 euros — nearly tripling the prior year. 2027: one million. 2028: 1.5 million. The three-year CAGR is 84%. That's not impossible in African luxury — it's a market growing at over 10% annually, in a region projected to double by 2031. Retbaa is positioning itself exactly where demand is arriving.

Speaker 1: What does 2026 actually look like quarter by quarter?

Speaker 2: It's a year that builds momentum. Q1 is preparation — laying foundations, converting the first major contracts. 22,000 euros projected. Then Q2 accelerates with the Africa CEO Forum, Ecobank partnerships — 133,000 euros. Q3 sees the Abidjan flagship taking shape, corporate contracts being signed ahead of year-end — 155,000 euros. And Q4 is the crescendo: flagship grand opening, Youth Olympic Games in Dakar, and the Majliss platform for corporate year-end gifting — 188,000 euros. The year ends with real momentum.

Speaker 1: The Youth Olympic Games in Dakar — that's a genuine accelerator for Retbaa?

Speaker 2: It's a window that any luxury brand would pay dearly for. A global event, in Massata's home city, drawing international decision-makers, media, and premium consumers. And Retbaa will be there.

Speaker 1: The big question now — margins. If revenue triples, does profitability hold?

Speaker 2: This is where the asset-light model becomes genuinely powerful. When fixed costs don't move and revenue triples, margins don't just hold — they improve. The projections: 25% EBITDA in 2026, 28% in 2027, over 30% by 2028. Net margin scales from 12% today to over 25%. Growth makes Retbaa more profitable, not less.

Speaker 1: And for an investor thinking about the exit — when, and how much?

Speaker 2: Exit projections sit in 2029-2030, with revenue of 2.5 to 3 million euros and EBITDA margins of 35 to 40%. At those levels, in the DTC luxury sector, valuation multiples produce a company worth 20 to 25 million euros. For current round investors, that's a potential return of 8 to 10 times their initial investment.

Speaker 1: That's what you'd call positive asymmetry — downside is limited because the company is already profitable, and upside potential is substantial.

Speaker 2: Exactly. This isn't a bet. It's a conviction built on real numbers, a documented market, and a founder who has already proven he can deliver.

Speaker 1: One sentence to close. Kemia.

Speaker 2: Retbaa turned 191,000 euros in year-one revenue into a trajectory toward 1.5 million by 2028 — with margins that grow, not shrink. That's rare. And the window is now.

Speaker 1: Thank you, Kemia. Next episode, we cover the market vision: why West Africa and the Gulf are exactly where luxury is being reinvented right now. See you then.

Speaker 2: See you then.
