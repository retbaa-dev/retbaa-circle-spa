# "The Abidjan Flagship: Retbaa's Most Important Milestone"
# Sources : Retbaa Review V10 (Strategic Roadmap 2026-2028), retbaa_investor_document_2026.md

---

Speaker 1: Welcome back to Retbaa Circle Insider. I'm Alex. Over the past two episodes, we've covered the financials and the market opportunity. Today we're getting concrete — about one specific milestone that Massata describes as the most important physical moment in Retbaa's next chapter. The Abidjan flagship.

Speaker 2: And I'm Kemia. I want to be precise here from the start: everything we're discussing today comes directly from the official strategic roadmap that Massata shared with investors. This isn't speculation — it's the plan.

Speaker 1: Let's set the scene first. Why Abidjan? Why not Lagos, why not Dakar, why not Paris?

Speaker 2: It's a deliberate, strategic choice — and when you understand the reasoning, it becomes obvious. Dakar is Retbaa's home. It's where Massata is from, where the brand's soul lives. But Dakar's retail infrastructure isn't yet positioned to carry a luxury flagship of the kind Retbaa needs. Paris is the brand's premium legitimacy anchor — but Paris is already saturated with luxury. Lagos is the largest economy in Africa, enormous potential — but it comes with complexity: regulatory, logistical, and reputational risks that are better approached once you have a proof of concept elsewhere. Abidjan is the answer because it's the economic capital of Francophone West Africa. It has a fast-growing affluent class, a strong commercial culture, direct connections to Dakar, Lagos, and Paris, and a luxury consumer who's been waiting for exactly what Retbaa offers.

Speaker 1: And when is this happening?

Speaker 2: The target is Q4 2026 — the fourth quarter. That's the strategic peak period: year-end, corporate gifting season, holiday momentum. Opening in Q4 isn't accidental — it's engineered so that the flagship's first weeks of operation coincide with the highest-spend period of the year.

Speaker 1: Walk us through what the flagship actually represents — physically and strategically.

Speaker 2: Physically, it's Retbaa's first owned retail space. Everything before this has been pop-ups — Paris, Dakar — and wholesale distribution through third-party retailers. The flagship is different. It's a space Retbaa controls completely. The scent when you walk in. The lighting. The sound. The way the products are presented. The conversation the sales team has with every customer. It becomes a total sensory environment — which is exactly what Retbaa's brand promise requires. You can't fully understand Retbaa without experiencing it with all your senses. The flagship makes that possible at scale.

Speaker 1: And strategically — what does this moment unlock?

Speaker 2: Three things. First, it signals permanence. Pop-ups are by definition temporary. A flagship says: we're here, we're serious, we're not going anywhere. That changes how distributors talk to you, how the press covers you, how potential partners approach you. Second, it becomes the conversion engine for the entire region. Every hotel, every corporate client, every institutional buyer in West Africa can now bring their contacts to a physical space — and that physical experience closes deals that no catalogue or digital presentation can close. The roadmap projects a 20 to 30 percent in-store conversion rate. That's not a projection — that's the expected performance of a well-executed luxury retail environment. Third, it gives Retbaa a regional stage. The flagship isn't just a store. It hosts events, tastings, private evenings for the investor circle and VIP clients. It becomes the cultural anchor of Retbaa in West Africa.

Speaker 1: You mentioned the Youth Olympic Games in Dakar — how does that connect to the flagship?

Speaker 2: The timing is extraordinarily fortunate. The Youth Olympic Games take place in Dakar in 2026 — they bring international media, decision-makers, athletes, sponsors from every continent. Retbaa will be positioned across the West African region exactly at that moment. Abidjan and Dakar are connected — economically, culturally, by air routes. When global attention lands on Dakar, it creates a halo over the entire region. The flagship in Abidjan benefits from that attention without being overwhelmed by it.

Speaker 1: Let's talk about the surrounding strategy — the roadmap mentions something called "Retbaa Moments." What is that?

Speaker 2: Retbaa Moments is the brand activation program that runs alongside the flagship. While the flagship is the permanent base, Retbaa Moments brings the brand to specific high-value locations and events. The roadmap specifically names: Sofitel Cotonou in Benin, activations in Lomé in Togo, and Accra in Ghana. These are pop-up experiences, tasting events, brand partnerships with luxury hospitality partners. The logic is elegant: the flagship anchors the brand in Abidjan, and Retbaa Moments radiates that presence outward across the sub-region. Every market that experiences Retbaa through a Moments activation becomes a potential expansion target for a future permanent presence.

Speaker 1: From a financial standpoint — what does the flagship contribute to the 2026 numbers?

Speaker 2: The Q4 contribution from the flagship and the Majliss platform combined represents 188,000 euros — nearly 38% of the full-year 2026 target of 500,000 euros. That concentration in Q4 is intentional. It's the window when corporate gifting peaks, when the Majliss premium gifting platform fires at full capacity, and when the flagship's grand opening drives maximum foot traffic and media coverage simultaneously. This is why the roadmap says: Q4 is the crescendo. Everything from Q1 through Q3 is preparation for that moment.

Speaker 1: What are the risks — and how does the roadmap address them?

Speaker 2: There are three risks named explicitly in the document. First: commercial pipeline delays. The mitigation is diversification — revenue doesn't depend on any single account or any single channel. B2B, retail, and the Majliss platform are three independent engines. If one slows, the others continue. Second: geographic execution risk. Operating across multiple West African markets simultaneously requires local expertise, local partnerships, and local cultural fluency. Retbaa addresses this through its SASU model — a plug-and-play legal and operational framework designed for rapid, standardized deployment across Francophone Africa. Third: supply chain. Retbaa mitigates this through direct relationships with tier-one artisan producers and flexible, premium local sourcing that bypasses global logistics bottlenecks.

Speaker 1: The flagship also has implications for how investors should think about valuation, doesn't it?

Speaker 2: Significantly. Right now, Retbaa's revenue is almost entirely B2B — wholesale and corporate. That's excellent for stability, but it doesn't capture the full valuation multiple of a luxury brand. Luxury brands that own retail — their own physical spaces — are valued at fundamentally higher multiples than those that distribute only through wholesale. The moment Retbaa has a flagship operating at the level the roadmap projects, it transforms from a "premium product company" to a "luxury brand with owned retail." In the DTC luxury valuation framework, that difference can be worth two to three times the multiple. It's not a minor detail — it's a step change in how the company is valued.

Speaker 1: One image to close. What does the Abidjan flagship look like in your mind?

Speaker 2: I see it at dusk. The light is that particular West African gold that doesn't exist anywhere else. You walk in through a door that smells faintly of the Brise de Dakar candle. The walls show the pink of the Lac Rose. There are no price tags visible — everything is presented as an object to discover, not a product to buy. A staff member brings you a tasting of three spices, asks you about your memories of food. And somewhere in the room, someone opens a box for the first time and finds something that makes them say: this is Africa. This is what I've been waiting for. That's the flagship. That's what Q4 2026 is building toward.

Speaker 1: Thank you, Kemia. Next episode — why building a community around Retbaa isn't a marketing exercise. It's the strategy. See you then.

Speaker 2: See you then.
